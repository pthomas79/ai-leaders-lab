Your first prompt
3
Have a conversation with Copilot
This is a back-and-forth conversation, not a questionnaire. The goal is alignment: Copilot understands your situation and you feel understood. The conversation is done when either Copilot says it has enough information, or you feel confident it gets what you need. A good move: ask Copilot to "play back what you've understood so far."
4
Refine until you're happy
Don't rush to the next step. Push back, challenge, and sharpen the content together. Try questions like:
  • How could this be better?
  • Let's roast this content we've been creating together
  • What assumption in this is most likely to be wrong?
  • Now that we have the full picture, what are some tensions, gaps, or opportunities to simplify?
  • Thoughts?
  • What else should I consider?
💡
Why this matters: This conversation creates alignment between you and the AI. Instead of guessing what you need from a single prompt, Copilot builds a shared understanding of your situation. That's the difference between using AI as a search engine and using it as a thinking partner. When you ask it to play back its understanding, you can catch misalignment before it produces anything.

In under 15 minutes, you went from a blank screen to a real deliverable — using three simple moves that work with any AI tool, for any task.

The method you just learned applies everywhere. Next time you use Copilot in Teams, Word, Outlook, or PowerPoint — start by setting context, let it interview you, then ask for a specific output. You'll get dramatically better results than prompting from scratch.
🧠 Pro Move
Save a summary so you can pick up where you left off
Before you close the conversation, ask Copilot: "Summarise this conversation and its outcomes so I can use it as a starting point next time." Save that summary in a Word document or in OneNote. Next time you start a new Copilot session on this topic, just drop that document into the chat. Copilot will pick up where you left off, with all the context from today. Think of it as a briefing note for your future self.
