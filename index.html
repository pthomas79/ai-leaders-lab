Step 3

Let Copilot interview you

This is the most powerful move in AI. Instead of trying to write the perfect prompt, you let Copilot ask you the right questions. Copy this prompt and paste it into your same conversation.

Your second prompt
Before creating anything, I'd like you to interview me so you fully understand what I need. Ask me questions one by one — give me numbered multiple-choice answers for each question, and include your recommendation. Once you've asked enough questions to feel confident, let me know you're ready to create my deliverable.
1
Answer each question Copilot asks you
Pick from the multiple-choice options or type your own answer. There's no wrong answer — just go with what feels right for your real situation.
2
Go through 4–6 questions
The interview usually takes 3–5 minutes. Each answer helps Copilot build a richer understanding of what you need.
3
When Copilot says it's ready, move to the next step
Copilot will tell you it has enough information. That's your cue to continue.
💡
Why this works: You just turned a one-way prompt into a two-way conversation. Copilot now has 10x more context than if you'd tried to write everything yourself. This is the difference between using AI as a search engine and using it as a thinking partner.

⚡ Bonus Prompt
Make it better
Once you have your first output, paste this to push it further. This is how you go from "good enough" to "impressive."
Review what you just created with a critical eye. What's missing? What could be stronger? What would a skeptical executive push back on? Give me your top 3 suggestions for improvement, then implement them all in an updated version.
🎯

You just did context engineering.

In under 15 minutes, you went from a blank screen to a real deliverable — using three simple moves that work with any AI tool, for any task.

  • Set your context — you told AI who you are and what you're working on
  • Let it interview you — you turned a prompt into a conversation
  • Constrained the output — you told it exactly what to produce
  • Iterated — you refined the output instead of starting over
🔑
The method you just learned applies everywhere. Next time you use Copilot in Teams, Word, Outlook, or PowerPoint — start by setting context, let it interview you, then ask for a specific output. You'll get dramatically better results than prompting from scratch.
🧠 Pro Move
Save your context for next time
Copy your entire conversation into a Word document and save it to a folder on SharePoint or OneDrive. Next time you work on this topic, paste it back in as your starting context. You just built a reusable "brain" for this project.
